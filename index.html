The Problem

Do you ever read the privacy policies for websites you visit or apps you use? In spite of the potential risks we face as companies collect and share our personal data, only 1 in 5 Americans say they always or often read these obscure documents.

Our Solution

Privacy Screen is a webtool that uses natural language processing and machine learning to assess a submitted privacy policy’s compliance with the new California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). The tool focuses on one key element of the CCPA: disclosing the user’s right to request deletion of their personal information.

Many privacy policies are lengthy, and the specific subsection addressing a user’s right to request deletion makes up a small percentage of the overall text. Policy Screen leverages an inventory of 200+ online privacy policies and uses these inputs to extract the subsection most pertinent to the user’s right to request deletion. This subsection is then fed to a Support Vector Machine (SVM) model in order to predict whether the extracted text is compliant with the CCPA’s deletion requirements.

After submitting a privacy policy, the user is presented with a simple assessment of compliance, accompanied by the specific portion of the text that was identified by the model as being most pertinent to the user’s right to request deletion. We hope you'll give Privacy Screen a try!
